## Learnings

- [2026-06-26 ~03:50] **Effort is the dominant lever; model is secondary.** Fidelity matrix against 6 known design-rationale gaps from the source session: Sonnet-high 2.5/6, Sonnet-max 4.5/6, Opus-high 2.5/6, Opus-max 6.0/6. At high effort, Sonnet and Opus tie; model only separates them once effort is maxed (then Opus +1.5). (0a64f481 · turn 310)
- [2026-06-26 ~03:50] The effort lever is nearly free: Sonnet-high→max gained +2.0 points at ~−$0.02 cost (~$0.90→$0.88). The model lever is expensive: Sonnet-max→Opus-max gained +1.5 points at +~$3.44 (~$0.88→$4.32). Spend effort first; upgrade model only after maxing effort. (0a64f481 · turn 310)
- [2026-06-26 ~03:50] Opus-max's ~2× larger output is *precision density, not bloat* — it carried the extra forks, ~40 distinct user quotes, and explicit decision branches that lower cells dropped; no repetition or padding found in the audit. (0a64f481 · turn 310)
- [2026-06-26 ~04:00] The **two-stage** pipeline (Haiku gather → capable synthesize) matched single-stage Opus-max on fidelity — structurally sound, and the basis for confining the cheap model to retrieval and the capable model to judgment. (0a64f481 · turn 310)
